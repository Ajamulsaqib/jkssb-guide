JKSSB Post Preference – Step by Step Guide

Post preference means selecting the order of posts you want to be considered for.

If you are eligible for multiple posts under one advertisement, JKSSB will consider your preference order during selection.

Higher preference = higher priority.

Preference option appears only if you are eligible for more than one post.

If only one post is applicable, preference selection may not be shown.

Select posts one by one in the order you want.

First selected post is your highest preference.

Make sure you understand the job role, cadre, and department before selecting preference.

Some advertisements allow changing preference order before final submission.

Use move up / move down options if available.

Once application is finally submitted, preference order usually cannot be changed.

Selecting a post without understanding its duties or location.

Giving wrong priority order.

Assuming preference does not matter (it does).

JKSSB considers preference only after merit.

Higher merit candidate will be considered first.

Preference does not guarantee selection if merit is low.

Recheck preference order carefully before final submission.

Wrong preference may result in allotment of an undesired post.

JKSSB does not allow changes after submission in most cases.
