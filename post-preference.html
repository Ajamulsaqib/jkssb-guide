How to Give Post Preference in JKSSB

Understand priority order to avoid wrong allotment

While applying for multiple posts on the portal of :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}, candidates are required to give their post preference. This decides which post will be allotted to you if you qualify for more than one.

What is Post Preference?

Post preference means the priority order of posts chosen by the candidate. The system considers your preference order first, then merit.

How to Give Preference (Step-by-Step)

  • All eligible posts will be shown on the screen
  • Arrange posts from most preferred to least preferred
  • Save preference carefully before final submission

Important Rules You Must Know

  • If you qualify for your first preference, other preferences are ignored
  • Once preference is locked, it cannot be changed
  • Merit is considered only within your chosen preference order

District vs UT Preference Strategy

  • District Cadre: Higher chance if local district
  • Division Cadre: Wider competition
  • UT Cadre: Maximum competition, higher cutoff
⚠️ Do not randomly arrange preferences. Think carefully about location, cadre, job role, and competition level.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Random preference order
  • Not saving preference
  • Ignoring cadre type
  • Assuming system will auto-correct
✅ Always re-check preference order during preview before final submission.
